Sheikh Mujibur Rahman

Introduction

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman (Bengali: শেখ মুজিবুর রহমান; 17 March 1920 – 15 August 1975), shortened as Sheikh Mujib or just Mujib, was a Bangladeshi politician and statesman. He is called the "Father of the Nation" in Bangladesh. He served as the first President of Bangladesh and later as the Prime Minister of Bangladesh from 17 April 1971 until his assassination on 15 August 1975.[1] He is considered to be the driving force behind the independence of Bangladesh. He is popularly dubbed with the title of "Bangabandhu" (Bôngobondhu "Friend of Bengal") by the people of Bangladesh. He became a leading figure in and eventually the leader of the Awami League, founded in 1949 as an East Pakistan–based political party in Pakistan. Mujib is credited as an important figure in efforts to gain political autonomy for East Pakistan and later as the central figure behind the Bangladesh Liberation Movement and the Bangladesh Liberation War in 1971. Thus, he is regarded "Jatir Janak" or "Jatir Pita" (Jatir Jônok or Jatir Pita, both meaning "Father of the Nation") of Bangladesh. His daughter Sheikh Hasina is the current leader of the Awami League and also the Prime Minister of Bangladesh.

Achivments

  • Led the Bengali nationalist movement and declared Bangladesh’s independence (1971).
  • Played the central role in the 1971 Liberation War that created Bangladesh.
  • Delivered the historic 7 March 1971 speech (a UNESCO Memory of the World item).
  • Became Bangladesh’s first prime minister (Jan 1972) and later president, helping rebuild the war-ravaged country.
  • Framed and enacted the 1972 Constitution of Bangladesh establishing a parliamentary system and fundamental rights.
  • Launched major administrative and legal reforms: reorganization of bureaucracy, repeal of colonial-era laws, and legal protections in the new constitution.
  • Implemented land reform and nationalization policies (banks, many industries) aiming to rebuild the economy and remove feudal remnants.
  • Expanded education and social rehabilitation programs for war victims, including large-scale primary-school efforts and relief for refugees and freedom fighters.
